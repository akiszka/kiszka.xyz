## Design Context

### Users
Tech peers, fellow founders, engineers, and recruiters — people who understand craft and can tell the difference between something genuinely well-made and something that just looks the part. They land on kiszka.xyz to get a read on Antoni: what he's built, what he cares about, whether he's someone worth reaching out to. They're evaluating signal, not scanning a resume.

### Brand Personality
**Playful, precise, grounded.** The desk metaphor is fun and tactile — it invites interaction — but the content, typography, and layout are disciplined. Nothing is accidental. The site should feel like opening a well-organized notebook that happens to have a few things you can fiddle with.

Core statement: *"I find beauty in simplicity."*

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Warm analog — cream paper, ink-black type, hard shadows, physical metaphors (tape, polaroids, desk objects). The visual language borrows from stationery and desktop ephemera, not screens.
- **Theme**: Light (warm cream). The viewing context is professional browsing — daytime, coffee in hand, evaluating a person. Dark mode would undercut the warmth.
- **Typography**: Serif display (large, confident name), geometric sans body (clean, modern), monospace labels (structured, data-like), cursive accents (personal, handwritten touch). Four fonts, each with a clear job.
- **Color**: Minimal warm neutrals. The palette is deliberately restrained — cream, warm black, muted gray, golden tape. The red live-dot is the only chromatic accent, used once. Color earns its place by being rare.
- **Interaction**: Draggable desk objects with spring physics and soft collision. The interaction model is exploratory — discover by touching, not by scrolling through sections.

**Anti-references** (explicitly avoid):
- Developer portfolio tropes: terminal themes, matrix rain, typed-text hero animations, "I'm a full-stack dev" declarations
- Startup landing pages: gradient heroes, metric dashboards, "trusted by" logo bars
- Generic AI output: cyan-on-dark, glassmorphism, gradient text, identical card grids

**References** (from the original design brief):
- sigilwen.ca — brutalist simplicity, almost-plain HTML
- theodore.net — animated headline, playful hero with scattered objects
- denka.me, jpge.space, aidanjs.com — personal sites with strong identity

### Design Principles

1. **Craft is the message.** The audience notices quality. Every spacing decision, easing curve, and type choice should reward close inspection. The site's memorability comes from polish, not gimmicks.

2. **Physical over digital.** The desk metaphor grounds the site in something tangible. Interactions should feel like moving real objects — weight, resistance, settling. Avoid anything that feels like a software UI (modals, tooltips, toggles).

3. **Restraint is playfulness.** The site is 35/100 on the playfulness dial — enough to invite exploration, not enough to feel like a toy. One interactive mechanic (the desk), executed well, beats five mediocre ones.

4. **Warmth through material.** The cream-and-ink palette, hard shadows, and serif headlines create warmth without being cute. The site should feel like quality paper, not a screen.

5. **Simplicity is earned.** "Beauty in simplicity" doesn't mean empty. It means every element present is necessary and considered. If something can be removed without loss, remove it.
